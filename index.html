About me

Welcome to my homepage! I am an Assistant Professor (chargé de cours) at the Montefiore Institute of Liège University. Prior to that, I was a Research Fellow affiliated with the ADAPT centre at Trinity College Dublin, and a part-time Assistant Professor affiliated with the WISE Lab of the Vrije Universiteit Brussel.

I conduct research in ontology and knowledge engineering, data modelling, Linked Data, and declarative approaches to data integration. I apply (and have applied) my research in various domains ranging from digital humanities and the cultural sector to e-commerce and geospatial data management.

In 2013, I obtained a PhD in computer science with a dissertation entitled "Grounding Ontologies with Social Processes and Natural Language". In my thesis, I investigated the role of the exchange of natural language definitions of concepts (called glosses) in the social interactions that take place when a community of stakeholders is collaboratively constructing ontologies. Before my PhD, I obtained a Bachelor in Applied Computer Science at Hogeschool Gent (University College Gent) in 2005, a BSc in Computer Science and an MSc in Applied Sciences and Engineering: Computer Science at the VUB in 2007 and 2009 respectively. You can find more information about my education and work experience on my LinkedIn page.
