Data Flowc back to ToC or Class ToC

IRI: https://w3id.org/dfd#DataFlow

A data flow can be best understood as data in motion, moving from one place in a system to another. A data flow is data that move together, so it can be composed of many individual pieces of data that are generated at the same time and that flow together to common destinations. (Source: J. A. Hoffer, J. F. George, and J. S. Valacich, “Modern Systems Analysis and Design,” Development, 2002) Examples of data include: data on a form or a report; results of a query to a database; or data entered in a computer.
